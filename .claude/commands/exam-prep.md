Create a complete exam preparation package for the subject in `$ARGUMENTS`.

Parse `$ARGUMENTS`:
- Subject only (e.g., `/exam-prep Corporate Finance`) → full exam prep
- Subject + focus (e.g., `/exam-prep Strategy Porter frameworks`) → focused on that area
- Subject + time (e.g., `/exam-prep Accounting 2 hours`) → condensed version for limited time

If `$ARGUMENTS` is empty, ask: "Which subject or exam are you preparing for?"

---

## Exam Prep Package Structure

### 1. Subject Map
A structured overview of everything that could appear on the exam. Organized by topic cluster. Flag which topics are high-frequency (likely to appear) vs. low-frequency (occasional).

### 2. Must-Know Concepts (Prioritized)
The 10-15 concepts that appear most often in MBA exams for this subject. For each:
- One-line definition
- The formula or model if applicable
- The classic exam question pattern
- The one thing students get wrong

### 3. Key Frameworks and Models
Every major framework the subject uses — presented as a quick-reference table:

| Framework | Created By | Core Logic | When to Use | Common Exam Angle |
|-----------|-----------|-----------|------------|------------------|

### 4. Formula Sheet (for quantitative subjects)
Every formula the student should know, with variable definitions and a note on when each is used.

### 5. Mock Exam
A realistic exam simulation:
- **Section A**: 5-10 short-answer or multiple-choice questions (20-30% of marks)
- **Section B**: 2-3 case/essay questions (70-80% of marks)
- Time allocation guidance
- Mark scheme / model answers provided separately (ask before revealing)

### 6. Exam Strategy
- How to approach different question types
- Common traps and how to avoid them
- Time management guidance
- How to structure written answers to maximize marks

### 7. Last 24 Hours Checklist
What to review in the final day. Prioritized by exam impact.

---

## Tone

Efficient and direct. The student is under time pressure. No filler. Prioritize ruthlessly — not everything is equally likely to appear.

## After Delivering

Ask: "Want to run through the mock exam, or should I drill you on any specific topic?"
