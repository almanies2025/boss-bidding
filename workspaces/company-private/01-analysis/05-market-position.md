# MBC Group — Market Position

## Overview

MBC Group occupies the dominant position in MENA media across three dimensions: free-to-air television, Arabic streaming, and Arabic news. Its competitive advantages are structural — built over 30+ years through exclusive content relationships, distribution scale, and deep audience familiarity. The group is now competing in the global streaming wars within the Arabic-language content universe.

## MBC's Position in MENA Television Advertising

| Market | MBC Claimed Share | Key Channels |
|--------|------------------|--------------|
| MENA TV advertising | ~45% | MBC1, MBC Drama, MBC4, Al Arabiya |
| Saudi TV advertising | Dominant (est. 50%+) | All Saudi-distributed channels |
| Egypt | Significant (via MBC Masr, MBC Masr 2) | Competes with Egyptian FTA channels |
| North Africa | Growing (MBC5 for Maghreb) | Limited vs. local broadcasters |

**Market concentration**: MBC Group, beIN Media Group, and OSN together commanded ~41% of total MENA media revenue in 2024. Within free-to-air TV advertising specifically, MBC's position is much higher — approaching monopoly in Saudi Arabia.

**Key competitors in FTA broadcasting:**
- **Rotana Group** (Saudi Arabia): Owned by Prince Alwaleed bin Talal; Rotana Music, Rotana Drama, Rotana Cinema, Rotana Khalijia. Focused on music and cinema; less general entertainment. Not directly competitive with MBC1 for breadth.
- **LBC (Lebanese Broadcasting Corporation)**: Lebanese private broadcaster; pan-Arab reach but much smaller. Historically strong for Lebanese/Levantine audience.
- **Al Jazeera** (Qatar): State-funded; primarily news; no direct FTA entertainment competition with MBC.
- **Egyptian state TV / MBC Masr competition**: Local Egyptian channels (ONtv, Al Nahar, DMC) compete with MBC Masr.

**Ramadan dominance**: MBC1 is typically #1 in Arab-world viewership during Ramadan, the Super Bowl equivalent for Arabic television. The network's Ramadan drama lineup drives both audience and advertising revenue concentration.

## MBC Shahid — Streaming Market Position

### MENA Streaming Landscape

| Platform | Type | SVOD Subscribers (2024 est.) | Market Share (SVOD) | Key Content |
|----------|------|------------------------------|----------------------|-------------|
| MBC Shahid | Arabic OTT | ~5M | #1 (~27%) | Arabic originals, MBC library |
| Netflix | Global OTT | ~3.7M MENA | #2–3 (~21%) | Int'l content + Arabic originals |
| OSN+ | Regional pay-TV OTT | ~1.5M | #3–4 | HBO, premium Western, limited Arabic |
| Disney+ / Star+ | Global OTT | ~1.5M est. | ~8-10% | Disney, Marvel, Star Wars, Pixar |
| StarzPlay (Lionsgate) | Global OTT | ~1M est. | ~5% | Action, Starz originals |
| Apple TV+ | Global OTT | Small | <3% | Premium originals; limited MENA marketing |

**AVOD leadership**: Shahid claims 46% AVOD market share in MENA — even more dominant on the free tier, where it competes directly with YouTube.

**Key differentiator**: Shahid's library of Arabic content — including MBC's 30+ year archive, exclusive Ramadan dramas, Turkish drama (dubbed), and MBC Studios originals — cannot be replicated by international platforms quickly or cheaply. This is the primary moat.

### Competitive Threats from International Platforms

- **Netflix**: Has invested in Arabic originals ("Jinn," "Paranormal") and acquired Arabic content licenses. Growing MENA subscriber base. The Netflix-Shahid bundle (July 2025) signals co-existence strategy rather than all-out war — Netflix needs Shahid's Arabic library; Shahid needs Netflix's brand for international subscribers.
- **Disney+**: Bundle with Shahid and OSN+ (December 2025) — similar logic. Disney is dominant in family content; limited Arabic competition with Shahid's drama focus.
- **YouTube**: Largest video platform in MENA; many Arabic content creators operate independently. Competes for AVOD attention (not SVOD).
- **TikTok**: Short-form; competes for younger audience attention; not directly competitive with long-form drama.

### The Bundle Strategy — MBC Shahid as Aggregator

The 2025 bundle deals position MBC Shahid as the **primary subscription aggregator** in MENA:

1. **Netflix + Shahid bundle (July 2025)**: Customers get both platforms under one billing relationship through MBC Now app; 21%+ saving vs. individual subscriptions
2. **Shahid + Disney+ + OSN+ bundle (December 2025)**: Three-platform bundle covering Arabic, Western premium (HBO via OSN), and Disney library under one Shahid subscription

This "super-bundle" strategy is analogous to Apple's approach (Apple One) or Amazon's channel model. It positions Shahid as the MENA hub, making it harder for subscribers to churn (losing Shahid means losing access to the bundle). This is a significant strategic move — Shahid is effectively inserting itself between the consumer and competing international platforms.

## Al Arabiya vs. Al Jazeera: News Competitive Position

| Metric | Al Arabiya | Al Jazeera |
|--------|-----------|-----------|
| Ownership | MBC Group / PIF (Saudi) | Qatar government |
| Launch | February 2003 | November 1996 |
| Headquarters | Riyadh (as of July 2025) | Doha, Qatar |
| Language | Arabic (+ Al Arabiya English) | Arabic (+ Al Jazeera English) |
| Editorial positioning | Pro-Saudi, Gulf states perspective | Historically independent; aligned with Qatari foreign policy |
| Estimated annual revenue | ~$94M | Estimated $500M–1B (heavily state-subsidized) |
| Global reach | 120+ countries | 170+ countries |
| Digital monthly visitors | ~45–80M (estimated) | ~100M+ |
| Criticism | Saudi propaganda arm | Qatar agenda; MB sympathies |

**Key dynamics:**
- Al Arabiya was launched explicitly as a "moderate" Arab news alternative to Al Jazeera, which Saudi Arabia viewed as too sympathetic to the Muslim Brotherhood and destabilizing to Gulf monarchies
- During the 2017–2021 Qatar blockade, Al Arabiya was a primary vehicle for Saudi anti-Qatar messaging
- Al Jazeera is better-funded (state subsidized, not required to be profitable) and has historically been seen as more credible by Western audiences
- Al Arabiya relocated fully to Riyadh in July 2025 — cementing its identity as a Saudi state media outlet
