# MBC Group — Business Model

## Overview

MBC Group operates a multi-revenue-stream media business spanning free-to-air broadcasting (primarily ad-supported), subscription streaming (Shahid/MBC Shahid), and content production/events. The company reports three distinct segments:

1. **BOCA (Broadcasting & Other Commercial Activities)** — Linear TV advertising + commercial services
2. **MBC Shahid** — OTT streaming (SVOD + AVOD)
3. **Media & Entertainment** — MBC Studios production, events, licensing, MBC Media Solutions

## Segment 1: BOCA — Broadcasting & Other Commercial Activities

### What It Is

BOCA is the traditional free-to-air broadcasting business. MBC's linear TV channels (MBC1, MBC2, MBC3, MBC Drama, etc.) and radio stations are free to air via satellite (Arabsat, Nilesat) and reach ~150 million viewers per week across MENA. Revenue is primarily advertising-based.

### Revenue Mechanics

- **Linear TV advertising**: Brands buy airtime across MBC channels. MBC1 is the flagship; Ramadan is the single most important commercial period — audience is 3–5x normal, ad rates spike dramatically
- **Digital advertising**: Growing overlay on linear; MBC Media Solutions manages inventory across broadcast and digital properties
- **Al Arabiya advertising**: Al Arabiya's ad sales managed by MBC Media Solutions under an exclusive partnership; cross-sells MBC's advertisers on news inventory
- **Sponsorships and branded content**: Show sponsorships (especially reality formats, talent competitions, major dramas) are a premium tier

### Market Position

- MBC claims ~45% share of MENA TV advertising market — a dominant position
- Major advertisers: FMCG multinationals (Procter & Gamble, Unilever), Saudi Aramco and affiliated entities, auto companies, telecom operators, government ministries
- Ramadan window is critical: Ramadan prime time on MBC1 is the highest-value advertising inventory in the Arab world
- FY 2025 BOCA revenue: SAR 2.83B; 22.7% more advertisers YoY in FY 2024

### Economics

- BOCA is the cash engine of MBC. Content is a cost (licensing Hollywood films for MBC2, commissioning Arabic dramas for Ramadan), but the marginal cost of each additional viewer is zero once content is acquired
- High operating leverage: fixed costs (content, transmission, staff) are largely fixed; advertising revenue is largely variable and cyclical
- Risks: economic downturns reduce advertiser spend; digital fragmentation erodes linear TV audiences long-term

## Segment 2: MBC Shahid — Streaming Platform

### What It Is

MBC Shahid (formerly Shahid, rebranded 2026) is the MENA region's leading streaming platform — operating as both:
- **SVOD (subscription)**: MBC Shahid Plus — paid tier with exclusive Arabic originals, early access to MBC dramas, sports content
- **AVOD (ad-supported)**: Free tier with MENA's largest ad-supported Arabic video library

### Revenue Mechanics

- **SVOD subscriptions**: Monthly/annual plans; prices typically $5–10/month in MENA markets
- **AVOD advertising**: Programmatic and direct-sold digital video advertising
- **Bundling revenue**: Revenue-share from Netflix bundle (July 2025) and Disney+/OSN+ bundle (December 2025) — bundles increase Shahid ARPU and reduce churn
- **Password-sharing crackdown (2025)**: Similar to Netflix's 2023 move; drove subscriber additions

### Strategic Pivot

Shahid is MBC's most strategically important asset for the next decade. The company has stated streaming is "becoming the core of its business." Key strategic elements:
- **Content exclusivity**: Arabic originals produced by MBC Studios premiere on Shahid first, driving SVOD subscriptions
- **Library size**: 9,000+ titles; Arabic content depth is the primary differentiation vs. Netflix/Disney+
- **Bundling**: Becoming a super-aggregator in MENA by bundling with Netflix (international) and Disney+/OSN+ (Western content)
- **International expansion**: Arabic diaspora in Europe, North America, and Australia is an undermonetized opportunity; MBC Shahid charges USD/EUR prices to diaspora subscribers
- **Break-even target**: Management guidance indicates 2027 break-even; losses narrowing materially (SAR 129M → SAR 79M in one year)

### Content Strategy

- **Arabic originals**: High-budget Ramadan drama series exclusive to Shahid; MBC Studios productions
- **Sports**: Selective sports rights (MBC Action acquired Formula One rights when beIN dropped them); Shahid integrates live sports
- **Licensed content**: International content licensed for MENA rights (Hollywood, Turkish drama — huge in Arabic MENA — Korean content)
- **User-generated/short-form**: Limited; focus on premium long-form

## Segment 3: Media & Entertainment

This is the fastest-growing segment (+69.6% YoY in FY 2025) and encompasses:

### MBC Studios (Content Production)

- **Nature**: MBC Studios is a production company creating content for MBC channels, Shahid, and third-party platforms
- **Revenue**: Content licensing, co-production fees, format sales
- **Pipeline**: ~100 productions; mix of Arabic dramas, films, reality formats
- **Hollywood co-productions**: "Desert Warrior" (with Anthony Mackie), Venice Film Festival premieres
- **Business model**: Content produced for internal use lowers content cost for BOCA/Shahid; third-party content sales generate licensing revenue
- **Strategic rationale**: Vertical integration — control the content supply chain; reduces dependence on expensive imported content; Arab original content is a strategic differentiator

### MBC Media Solutions

- Commercial and advertising sales house for MBC channels and Al Arabiya
- Exclusive deal to sell Al Arabiya News Network advertising inventory
- Represents cross-platform bundled advertising packages

### Events and Other

- Live events in Saudi Arabia (concerts, cultural events, gaming events tied to Vision 2030)
- Likely beneficiary of Saudi Arabia's 2024–2025 entertainment events boom (commercial registrations in arts/entertainment +20% YoY in 2024)

## Content Cost Structure

MBC's content strategy is a mix of owned, licensed, and commissioned:

| Content Type | Example | Cost Model |
|-------------|---------|-----------|
| Commissioned Arabic originals | Ramadan drama series | High upfront; shared revenue on Shahid |
| Hollywood licensed (MBC2, Max) | Major studio films | Volume licensing deals; per-title fees |
| MBC Studios owned productions | "Rashash," "HWJN" | Capex; monetized across channels + Shahid + licensing |
| Turkish drama (dubbed) | Hugely popular on MBC4, Drama | Per-episode licensing; low cost relative to ratings value |
| Korean/international drama | Growing library | Similar to Turkish |
| Sports rights | Formula One (MBC Action), selective | Annual rights fees |
| News production (Al Arabiya) | In-house | Staffing + bureaus globally |

## Advertising Business Deep Dive

- **Ramadan concentration risk**: A significant portion of MBC's annual advertising revenue is earned during Ramadan (typically 30 days). Advertisers spend disproportionately in this window because Arab viewership peaks. An economic shock during Ramadan (or weak advertiser demand) materially impacts annual results.
- **MENA advertiser base**: MBC's advertiser mix is dominated by Saudi Arabia, UAE, Kuwait, and Egypt — the wealthiest Arab economies. Saudi Arabia alone is ~39% of the MENA media/entertainment market.
- **Duopoly-ish dynamics**: In Arabic free-to-air, MBC and Rotana (a Saudi group separately owned by Prince Alwaleed bin Talal) are the two dominant groups. MBC's claimed 45% share implies Rotana, MBC, and a few others split the bulk of the market.

## Key Business Model Risks

1. **Ramadan concentration**: ~30-40% of annual ad revenue in one month
2. **Advertiser cyclicality**: MENA advertising tracks oil prices and GCC government spending
3. **Streaming losses**: Shahid remains loss-making (SAR 78.7M in FY 2025); path to profitability not guaranteed
4. **Content cost inflation**: Arabic originals cost rising as competition for premium writers/directors/talent increases
5. **Platform disintermediation**: Netflix, YouTube increasingly dominate younger MENA audiences; free-to-air viewership eroding secularly
6. **Ad market structural shift**: Dollars shifting from linear TV to digital (Meta, Google, TikTok) even in MENA
