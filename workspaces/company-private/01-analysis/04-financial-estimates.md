# MBC Group — Financial Estimates

## Important Caveat

MBC Group has been listed on the Tadawul (Saudi Exchange, ticker: 4072) since January 2024 and files quarterly earnings releases. The company reports revenue, gross profit, and net profit by segment. However:

- **EBITDA is not explicitly reported** — must be estimated from available data
- **Segment-level margins** are partially disclosed
- **Shahid unit economics** (e.g., CAC, churn, LTV) are not disclosed
- **Al Arabiya financials** are not separately disclosed (only estimated from third-party sources)
- Historical pre-IPO financials are incomplete; pre-2022 revenue is not publicly available in audited form

## EBITDA Estimation

MBC does not disclose EBITDA directly. The following is triangulated from gross profit margins and industry benchmarks:

### Gross Profit Data (Available)

| Year | Revenue (SAR M) | Gross Profit (SAR M) | Gross Margin |
|------|-----------------|----------------------|--------------|
| FY 2022 | ~2,560 | ~640 | ~25% |
| FY 2023 | 3,703 | 921 | 24.9% |
| FY 2024 | 4,196 | 1,204 | 28.7% |

### EBITDA Proxy

BOCA (broadcasting) businesses in emerging markets typically show EBITDA margins of 25–40% once at scale. Streaming (Shahid) is loss-making, depressing group EBITDA. Media & Entertainment (production) is capital-intensive with thinner margins.

Estimated group-level EBITDA (FY 2025):

| Component | Estimate |
|-----------|---------|
| BOCA EBITDA (est. 35% margin on SAR 2,831M) | SAR ~990M |
| Shahid EBITDA (loss; SAR 78.7M net loss + D&A est.) | SAR (~100-150M) |
| Media & Entertainment EBITDA (est. 10-15% margin) | SAR ~120-175M |
| Corporate/overhead (unallocated) | SAR (~80-120M) |
| **Group EBITDA (estimated)** | **SAR ~760–900M** |
| **Group EBITDA margin (est.)** | **~14–17%** |

These are bottom-up estimates, not disclosed figures. Cross-check: SAR 437.5M net profit + est. D&A ~SAR 150-200M + est. finance costs ~SAR 100M + taxes ~SAR 80M = ~SAR 767–817M EBITDA. This is consistent with the 14–17% range above.

- SVOD: SAR 1,080M / 5M average subscribers ≈ SAR 216/subscriber/year ≈ $57/year ≈ $4.75/month
- This ARPU is low relative to Netflix globally (~$13/month) but appropriate for MENA price points
- International diaspora subs pay more (USD/EUR pricing); growth in this segment improves ARPU mix

## Al Arabiya Financial Estimates

Al Arabiya's financials are not separately disclosed by MBC. Third-party sources:

- Annual revenue estimate: ~$93.6M (~SAR 350M) per RocketReach/industry data (2025)
- Business model: 100% advertising-funded; no subscription revenue
- MBC Media Solutions manages all advertising inventory
- Likely included within the BOCA segment in group reporting

Al Arabiya's revenue is smaller than comparable international news channels (Al Jazeera Group, funded by Qatar state, does not report revenue). As a primarily free-to-air news channel targeting pan-Arab and English-language audiences, Al Arabiya is more of a strategic/political asset than a major profit center.

**Key observation**: MBC trades at a premium to Western linear TV companies, reflecting:
1. Higher revenue growth rate (28.5% vs. flat or negative for ITV/ProSieben)
2. Saudi stock market premium (Vision 2030 narrative)
3. Streaming optionality (Shahid)
4. Emerging market growth premium

Implied EV at current market cap + estimated net debt:
- Market cap (SAR 10.25B) ≈ $2.74B
- Net debt: estimated modest (MBC has historically had limited leverage); actual figure not disclosed
- Enterprise value: ~$2.74–3.0B (enterprise value roughly approximates market cap at low leverage)

At SAR 5.4B revenue (~$1.44B) and estimated EBITDA ~SAR 780–900M (~$208–240M):
- EV/Revenue: ~2.0x
- EV/EBITDA (at current market price): ~11–13x
- EV/EBITDA (at PIF's implied whole-company value of ~$3.7B): ~15–18x

The PIF control premium of ~18–20x EBITDA is a reasonable premium for a dominant regional media company with strong streaming growth.
