# MBC Group — Strategic Opportunities

## Context

With PIF as majority shareholder and Shahid growing rapidly, MBC Group is at a strategic inflection point. The company has the dominant market position, sovereign backing, a growing streaming platform, and a production studio — but it has not yet achieved full-value realization of its streaming optionality, international diaspora reach, or content library depth. Several strategic paths are available to create significant value.

---

## 1. Shahid International Expansion — Arabic Diaspora

### Opportunity

25–30 million Arabic speakers live outside MENA. The Arabic diaspora is concentrated in:
- **France, Germany, Netherlands, Belgium** — North African diaspora (Moroccan, Algerian, Tunisian)
- **UK, Sweden, Denmark** — Iraqi, Syrian, Egyptian communities
- **USA, Canada** — Mixed Arab diaspora
- **Australia** — Lebanese-Australian community

These subscribers pay USD/EUR pricing (~$8–15/month) vs. MENA pricing (~$4–5/month), representing a 2–4x ARPU premium. MBC Shahid is currently available internationally but is not aggressively marketed in diaspora markets.

### Strategic Action

- Launch localized marketing campaigns targeting diaspora communities in Western Europe and North America
- Partner with telcos and pay-TV operators in those markets for bundled distribution (similar to the Netflix/Disney+ bundle model used in MENA)
- Create diaspora-specific content tiers (e.g., North African content for French/Belgian market)
- Potential revenue uplift: even 1M additional international subscribers at $10/month ARPU = $120M/year incremental revenue

### Precedent

Eros Now (Indian diaspora streaming), Viu (Southeast Asian diaspora), and Hotstar (South Asian diaspora via Disney+) demonstrate the international monetization of diaspora OTT content.

---

## 2. Shahid Monetization Scaling — Password-Sharing, Ad-Tier, Bundling

### Opportunity

Shahid's AVOD tier has 18.6M monthly active users but is monetized at significantly lower rates than SVOD. Three immediate opportunities:

1. **Password-sharing crackdown (ongoing)**: Netflix's 2023 playbook — enforce one account = one household — drove material subscriber additions. MBC ran this initiative in 2025 and saw subscriber growth. Further enforcement could convert millions of password-sharers.

2. **Ad-supported tier optimization**: 18.6M AVOD users generating limited ad revenue. Premium ad products (branded content, interactive ads, Ramadan specials) are undermonetized. Programmatic ad tech investment could double AVOD ARPU.

3. **Bundle as a retention moat**: The Netflix + Shahid + Disney+ + OSN+ bundle ecosystem positions MBC Shahid as the hub subscription. Subscribers who bundle are significantly less likely to churn. This is a durable competitive advantage that should be aggressively expanded.

---

## 3. MBC Studios — Third-Party Content Sales and Co-Production

### Opportunity

MBC Studios has ~100 productions in its pipeline and is already co-producing with Hollywood studios. The strategic opportunity is to position MBC Studios as the **Arabic content studio of record** for global platforms:

- **Netflix Arabic originals**: Netflix is investing in MENA originals but lacks production infrastructure. MBC Studios could be a primary production partner (similar to Studio Dragon in Korea producing for Netflix)
- **Format sales**: Arabic reality TV formats adapted for Middle Eastern or international markets
- **Co-production with Turkish studios**: Turkish drama is hugely popular in the Arab world; an MBC-Turkey co-production slate could be distributed globally via Shahid + Netflix
- **Hollywood production pipeline**: "Desert Warrior" showed MBC can operate at international quality standards

### Revenue potential

If MBC Studios transitions from primarily internal content production to a third-party studio model (15–20% of revenue from external content sales), it adds a high-margin licensing revenue stream. At SAR 5.4B group revenue, even a 5% studios licensing contribution = SAR 270M.

### Precedent

Indian OTT studios (Dharmatic, Applause Entertainment), Korean production companies (Studio Dragon, JTBC Studios), and Israeli format exporters (Armoza Formats) demonstrate the global export model for non-English content.

## 4. M&A — Consolidation of MENA Media

### Opportunity

The MENA media landscape remains fragmented. With PIF backing, MBC has the capital and strategic mandate to consolidate:

**Potential M&A targets:**
| Target | Rationale | Challenge |
|--------|-----------|-----------|
| Rotana Group | Complementary channels (music, cinema); owned by Prince Alwaleed | Political — Alwaleed unlikely to sell at current valuations |
| OSN+ streaming | Would eliminate key streaming competitor; adds HBO access to Shahid | OSN majority-owned by PE; deal structure complex |
| StarzPlay MENA | Lionsgate content; subscriber addition | Lionsgate control; integration risk |
| LBC (Lebanese) | Lebanese audience; Levantine content library | Lebanon political risk; small scale |
| Anghami | Music streaming (Arab world equivalent of Spotify) | Listed on Nasdaq; market cap ~$50M (small but strategic) |
| Egyptian production companies | Content access in Egypt (100M+ Arabic speakers) | Fragmented; talent-dependent |

### Most Plausible M&A

An OSN+ acquisition or merger would be the most strategically significant. OSN+ gives Shahid HBO/Warner content rights plus a subscriber base, creating a stronger competitive moat vs. Netflix. The Disney+/OSN+/Shahid bundle (December 2025) is already a step toward deeper integration.

## 5. Gaming and Interactive Entertainment

### Opportunity

The NEOM-MBC joint venture for a AAA game development studio is a first move. Larger opportunity:

- **Esports broadcasting**: Leverage MBC channels and Shahid to broadcast Saudi esports events (Esports World Cup, Savvy Games events)
- **Gaming content programming**: Create gaming content on MBC channels and Shahid for the 70%+ of Saudi youth who play video games
- **Game licensing deals**: MBC Shahid could secure streaming rights for major esport events globally
- **Character/IP licensing**: MBC's existing Arabic TV characters and franchises as the basis for mobile games

Saudi Arabia spent ~$38B on gaming sector development under Vision 2030. MBC is positioned to capture the media/broadcast layer of this spending.

---

## 6. Technology and AI Integration

### Opportunity

- **AI dubbing/localization**: MBC brings enormous amounts of international content (Hollywood, Turkish, Korean) to Arabic audiences via dubbing. AI dubbing tools could reduce localization costs by 50–70%, dramatically expanding the volume of international content MBC can affordably license and air.
- **AI-driven content recommendation**: Shahid's recommendation engine quality directly affects AVOD/SVOD engagement and churn. Investment in AI personalization is table stakes but remains an area for improvement.
- **AI news production (Al Arabiya)**: Automated news summaries, AI-assisted journalism, real-time translation — all can reduce Al Arabiya's operational costs.
- **Synthetic media/deepfake risk**: As Saudi Arabia's state broadcaster, MBC is also a potential target of synthetic media attacks. Investment in content authentication is both a risk mitigation and a potential commercial product.

---

## 7. Film Industry — Saudi Cinema Buildout

Saudi Arabia went from zero cinemas (35-year ban lifted in 2018) to 66+ cinemas by 2024. The country is investing heavily in developing a domestic film industry. MBC Studios is positioned to:

- **Produce Saudi feature films** for domestic theatrical release and international festival circuit
- **Secure theatrical distribution deals** for MBC Studios productions
- **Partner with Hollywood studios** on Saudi/Arab co-productions that qualify for Saudi government content funding

This is still early-stage but represents a genuine greenfield opportunity as Saudi film evolves from zero to a mature industry within the Vision 2030 timeline.

---

## 8. Al Arabiya — Credibility Investment and Monetization

Al Arabiya's political alignment with Saudi Arabia limits its credibility with Western audiences and international advertisers. However, there is a strategic case for:

- **Digital expansion**: Al Arabiya English has underinvested in digital-first journalism; there is room to build a BBC Arabic/France 24 competitor in English-language coverage of MENA
- **Digital advertising**: International digital ad rates (CPMs) are 3–5x MENA rates; growing Al Arabiya English's Western digital traffic creates a higher-value advertising segment
- **Subscription news**: Premium news subscription (similar to The Economist or Financial Times) for regional policy/business professionals — niche but high-value

The credibility constraint is real. Al Arabiya's value is primarily political (Saudi soft power), not commercial. However, under PIF ownership, there may be appetite to invest in its international credibility as a Saudi soft-power tool.
