# MBC Group — Vision 2030 Opportunity

## Saudi Arabia's Vision 2030: The Entertainment Dimension

Saudi Arabia's Vision 2030 program, launched in 2016 under Crown Prince MBS, targets economic diversification away from oil dependency. The entertainment, media, and creative economy sectors are explicit pillars of this plan.

### Key Vision 2030 Entertainment Targets

| Target | 2016 Baseline | 2030 Goal |
|--------|--------------|-----------|
| Household entertainment spending | 2.9% of household expenditure | 6.0% |
| Entertainment sector GDP contribution | ~SAR 16B ($4.3B) | SAR 47B ($12.5B) |
| Jobs in entertainment sector | — | 450,000 jobs created |
| Cinemas in Saudi Arabia | 0 (banned until 2018) | 300+ screens |
| Gaming companies (Saudi HQ) | — | 250+ companies |

### Saudi Entertainment Market Growth

- Saudi Arabia's entertainment and amusement market: $2.46B (2024) → $6.10B (2033), CAGR 10.6%
- Commercial registrations in Saudi arts/entertainment: +20% YoY in 2024
- General Entertainment Authority (GEA) actively regulating and promoting the sector

## MBC's Structural Alignment with Vision 2030

MBC Group is not just a beneficiary of Vision 2030 — it is a designated instrument of Saudi cultural policy. With PIF as 54% shareholder, MBC is now explicitly part of the Vision 2030 execution apparatus.

### Alignment Points

| Vision 2030 Goal | MBC Role |
|-----------------|---------|
| Build Saudi creative economy | MBC Studios producing Saudi content; MBC Academy training local talent |
| Develop entertainment infrastructure | MBC relocated HQ to Riyadh; Al Arabiya to Riyadh; building media industry cluster |
| Increase household entertainment spending | MBC channels + Shahid are primary entertainment vectors for Saudi households |
| Export Saudi/Arab culture globally | Shahid international expansion; MBC Studios global co-productions |
| Attract foreign investment to Saudi media | IPO on Tadawul draws global institutional capital |
| Diversify media ownership (domestic) | MBC as model of "national champion" media company |
| Support Saudi gaming/esports | NEOM-MBC JV for AAA game development studio |
| Sports entertainment | MBC Action acquired Formula One rights; selective sports on Shahid |

## Key Opportunity Areas

### 1. Advertising Market Growth (BOCA)

The MENA advertising market is projected to double from ~$8.7B (2024) to $17.4B (2029). Saudi Arabia's share is growing disproportionately as Vision 2030 drives consumer spending and domestic business formation.

**MBC's position**: As the dominant FTA broadcaster (~45% TV ad market share), MBC is the primary beneficiary of any uplift in MENA advertising spend. Government-linked advertisers (Saudi Aramco, STC, banks, tourism boards) are significant direct or indirect spend drivers.

Saudi tourism campaigns — Neom, Red Sea Project, Diriyah, AlUla — represent a new and growing category of government advertiser that MBC is uniquely positioned to capture.

### 2. MBC Shahid — Domestic Streaming Penetration

Saudi Arabia's young, highly connected population (median age ~27; smartphone penetration ~97%) is ideal for streaming growth. Vision 2030 infrastructure investment (5G, fiber to home) is expanding broadband access.

- Current Shahid SVOD penetration in Saudi Arabia: estimated 5–10% of households (based on ~5M subscribers in MENA vs. estimated 8–10M Saudi households)
- Comparable markets (Indonesia, India) have seen streaming penetration reach 20–30%+ at similar economic development stages
- The Saudi government's push to keep Arabic content on Saudi platforms (rather than ceding to Netflix/Disney+) implicitly advantages MBC Shahid

### 3. Ramadan as a Growth Window

Ramadan viewership and advertising concentration will grow as Saudi Arabia's entertainment sector expands. The Ramadan premium — already 3–5x normal audience — may become even more pronounced as production values increase and international audiences tune in via Shahid.

**The Ramadan content arms race**: MBC is investing more in original Ramadan drama productions each year. As streaming becomes the primary Ramadan viewing window (live via Shahid), MBC can charge premium SVOD subscription rates for Ramadan access.

### 4. MBC Studios — Content Export

Saudi Arabia's Vision 2030 includes ambitions to export Saudi culture and soft power globally. MBC Studios is the primary vehicle for:

- **Arabic originals for global distribution**: International co-productions with Hollywood studios position Saudi-backed content on global platforms
- **Format exports**: Arabic reality TV formats, talent competitions adapted for international markets
- **Film**: Saudi Arabia held its first cinematic releases in 2018 (end of 35-year ban); MBC Studios is a natural production partner for the nascent Saudi film industry
- **Venice Film Festival credibility**: MBC Studios premiered "The Voice of Hind Rajab" at Venice — signaling ambition for international film prestige, though also raising political controversy

### 5. Gaming and Esports

The NEOM-MBC Group joint venture to establish a AAA game development studio is notable:

- Saudi Arabia's National Gaming and Esports Strategy targets 250 gaming companies by 2030
- Esports World Cup (Riyadh) offered a record $70M prize pool in 2024 — cementing Saudi Arabia as global esports hub
- MBC's media infrastructure (channels + Shahid) provides natural broadcast/streaming distribution for gaming content
- Untapped monetization: gaming content on Shahid, gaming advertising on BOCA channels

### 6. Saudi Sports Rights — Complex Dynamics

MBC has selectively acquired sports rights:
- **Formula One**: MBC Action acquired F1 broadcast rights after beIN did not renew — a high-profile win for free-to-air sports
- **Saudi Pro League (football)**: MBC declined to renew SPL rights despite the league's massive investment in international stars (Ronaldo, Benzema, Neymar). This is a significant strategic choice — prioritizing commercial discipline over sports rights inflation
- **Strategic question**: If Saudi Pro League viewership grows (driven by star power and Vision 2030 football ambitions), MBC's decision to exit SPL rights could prove costly; alternatively, it avoids the ruinous rights inflation seen in European football

### 7. International Diaspora Monetization

~25–30 million Arabic speakers live outside the MENA region (primarily Europe, North America, Australia). MBC Shahid is increasingly targeting this diaspora:

- Higher ARPU: diaspora pays USD/EUR pricing vs. MENA pricing (potentially 2–3x ARPU premium)
- Lower content cost per subscriber: incremental delivery cost of streaming is minimal
- Bundle deals with Netflix/Disney+ make Shahid more attractive to diaspora (one subscription covers Arabic + international content)

The Arabic diaspora opportunity is underpenetrated and represents a path to materially higher Shahid ARPU.

### 8. Saudi Arabia as a Media Hub — Regulatory Tailwinds

Saudi Arabia's government has actively pushed international media companies to relocate their MENA HQs to Riyadh (the "HQ Requirement" policy). This creates:

- Concentration of media professionals and talent in Riyadh — benefits MBC Academy and talent pipeline
- Infrastructure investment in media production facilities
- Regulatory clarity and state support for MBC as the "home team"

## Vision 2030 Risks to MBC

While Vision 2030 is largely tailwind, there are counterbalancing risks:

| Risk | Mechanism |
|------|-----------|
| Government pressure on content | State ownership means Vision 2030 content priorities may override commercial decisions |
| Competitive crowding | Government is attracting multiple international media players to Saudi Arabia; MBC loses uniqueness |
| Mega-project competition for entertainment spend | Neom, Red Sea Project compete with traditional media for consumer entertainment time |
| Sports rights cost inflation | Saudi government spending drives up sports rights prices; MBC faces pressure to compete |
| Succession/political risk | MBC's fate is tied to PIF and MBS; political change could alter priorities |

---

## Financial Opportunity — Vision 2030 Upside Case

If Saudi media GDP grows from SAR 16B ($4.3B) in 2024 to SAR 47B ($12.5B) by 2030 as targeted, and MBC maintains its ~35–40% share of the Saudi media market:

- Implied MBC revenue from Saudi market alone: SAR 16–19B ($4.3–5.1B) by 2030
- Adding Egypt, UAE, and other MENA markets + international Shahid: total group revenue could reach SAR 20–25B ($5.3–6.7B) by 2030
- This represents a 3–4x revenue increase from current SAR 5.4B levels over 5 years

This is a bull-case scenario requiring all tailwinds to materialize. A more conservative forecast (15–20% annual growth) implies SAR 10–14B by 2030.
