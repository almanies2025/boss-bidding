## A. Saudi Aramco - Income Statement

| Metric | FY2023 | FY2024 | FY2025 |
|--------|--------|--------|--------|
| **Revenue** | 529.5 | 494.0 | 465.0 |
| **Cost of Revenue** | (317.0) | (302.0) | (290.0) |
| **Gross Profit** | 212.5 | 192.0 | 175.0 |
| **EBITDA** | 249.0 | 228.0 | 210.0 |
| **Depreciation & Amortization** | (42.0) | (44.0) | (46.0) |
| **EBIT (Operating Income)** | 207.0 | 184.0 | 164.0 |
| **Net Finance Cost** | (3.5) | (5.0) | (6.5) |
| **Income Tax (Zakat & Tax)** | (96.0) | (83.0) | (72.0) |
| **Net Income** | 121.3 | 106.2 | 97.0 |
| **EPS (USD)** | 0.496 | 0.434 | 0.397 |

**Notes:**
- Aramco's effective tax rate is approximately 50% (combination of income tax and royalties to the Saudi government). This is among the highest in the sector and is a structural feature, not a cyclical one.
- Revenue decline from FY2023 to FY2025 driven primarily by lower realized oil prices (Brent averaging ~$82/bbl in 2023, ~$78 in 2024, ~$73 in 2025) and voluntary OPEC+ production cuts.
- FY2025 net income of ~$97B still makes Aramco the most profitable company in the world by a wide margin.

| **Net Debt / Equity** | 0.10x | 0.19x | 0.27x |
| **Net Debt / EBITDA** | 0.12x | 0.23x | 0.35x |

**Notes:**
- Balance sheet is exceptionally strong but debt has been rising to fund dividends, CapEx, and the Sabic acquisition payments. Gross debt grew from $81B to $110B over three years.
- Equity declining as dividends paid exceed retained earnings -- a trend that bears watching if oil prices remain depressed.
- Net Debt/EBITDA at 0.35x is still extraordinarily low vs peers (most are 0.5-1.5x), but the upward trajectory is notable.

## C. Saudi Aramco - Cash Flow Statement

| Metric | FY2023 | FY2024 | FY2025 |
|--------|--------|--------|--------|
| **Operating Cash Flow** | 181.0 | 163.0 | 148.0 |
| **Capital Expenditures** | (49.7) | (53.0) | (55.0) |
| **Free Cash Flow** | 131.3 | 110.0 | 93.0 |
| **Dividends Paid** | (98.0) | (124.0) | (112.0) |
| **Share Buybacks** | 0 | 0 | 0 |
| **FCF - Dividends** | 33.3 | (14.0) | (19.0) |
| **Dividend Payout (% of FCF)** | 75% | 113% | 120% |

**Notes:**
- **Critical finding:** Since FY2024, Aramco's dividends have exceeded free cash flow. The company is funding dividends partly through debt issuance. This is unsustainable at current oil prices without either (a) oil price recovery, (b) dividend cut, or (c) continued balance sheet deterioration.
- The base dividend (~$78B) is sustainable; the performance-linked dividend (~$34-46B) is the variable component that creates the shortfall.
- CapEx is rising as Aramco invests in gas expansion (Jafurah field), downstream (Ras Al-Khair), and low-carbon initiatives.
