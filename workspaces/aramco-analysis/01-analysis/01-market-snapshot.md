## Key Observations

1. **Aramco trades at a significant premium** to Western majors on EV/EBITDA (7.5x vs peer average of ~4.8x) and P/E (15.8x vs peer average of ~10.1x). This premium reflects reserve scale, low-cost production, and sovereign backing, but also illiquidity due to low free float.

2. **Dividend yield is competitive** at 6.5%, the highest in the group, but this is partially a function of Saudi government fiscal needs rather than pure capital allocation discipline. The sustainability of the dividend at lower oil prices is a key risk.

3. **Market cap dominance is misleading** given that only ~1.7% of shares trade freely. True float-adjusted market cap is approximately USD 30 billion, which is smaller than BP.

4. **European majors (SHEL, TTE, BP) trade at steep discounts** to US majors (XOM, CVX), reflecting investor skepticism about energy transition strategies, lower buyback commitments, and European regulatory risk.

5. **Beta:** Aramco's low beta (0.50) reflects the fixed peg, government ownership stability, and limited free float. It does not reflect true business risk, which is heavily correlated to oil prices.
